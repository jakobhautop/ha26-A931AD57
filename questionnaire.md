Which attack vector did you choose and why?

But it was hard to decide which one to pick. So i dedicated 2 hours for each task on 3 different days
to try them out. 

I had previously watched videos on reverse engineering binaries and i ended up choosing netup because of 
that aspect. To me it was the most challenging task and thats why i picked it. Since this test might be the

Was the difficulty and technical level of the test in line with your
expectations? Please explain.

I was not sure what to expect honestly. The initial task reading the .u5fs image felt like

coding-interview challenge. This was okay but not overly exciting. The other challenge where we 
had to attack the DDING-3000 that was awesome. 

How hard did you find the test? Was there a smooth progression in the difficulty
of the tasks?

Like i said first part was very chill. That just felt like work that had to be done. 
Since i did not complete the task i chose I can only say i found it very difficult :) 

But i do not mind tasks being very challenging. I look at it like its just how the task would
work in reality. Like imagine we exfiltrated firmware from an iranian or russian drone? Prob that
does not come with manual either and so what.. It has to be hard thats fine :) I feel like
we need people who can work around situations like that. Skillwise im not there yet, unfortunately.
